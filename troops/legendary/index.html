What Are Legendary Troops in Empires & Puzzles?

Legendary troops are the highest rarity (5★) troops in Empires and Puzzles, introduced in Version 63 (January 2024). They represent the pinnacle of troop power and feature a unique class synergy system that gives bonus stats when paired with heroes of the matching class.

Each legendary troop has a primary class and a secondary class. When equipped on a hero whose class matches the primary or secondary class, additional stat bonuses activate. There are 10 different class combinations per element (50 in Set 1, 50 in Set 2 — 100 total legendary troops in the game).

Beyond class bonuses, every legendary troop also has a unique passive skill that provides permanent effects like increased special damage, reduced status ailment damage, extra healing, critical chance bonus, or resistance to debuffs. These passives make legendary troops a significant upgrade over epic troops.

Class Synergy — Primary & Secondary Bonuses

The class synergy system is what makes legendary troops truly special. When you equip a legendary troop on a hero whose class matches the troop's primary class, you gain significant bonus stats to attack, defense, and health. The secondary class also provides bonuses, typically at lower values.

The 10 hero classes in E&P are: Wizard, Ranger, Paladin, Druid, Barbarian, Monk, Sorcerer, Rogue, Fighter, and Cleric. Each legendary troop pairs two of these classes. For example, a Wizard/Ranger troop would give its best bonuses and passive skill to Wizard and Ranger heroes, and base stats only to all other classes.

This means optimizing your legendary troop usage requires matching troops to heroes by both element and class. The troop grid above lets you browse all legendary troops — click any troop to see its exact class pairing, passive skill, and stat growth at every level.

Legendary Troop Passive Skills

Every legendary troop comes with one of 10 possible passive skills:

  • •Critical Modifier — 15% critical chance bonus
  • •Increased Special Damage — +8% direct special damage
  • •Special Damage Reduction — -8% special damage taken
  • •Status Ailment Damage Reduction — -10% damage from ailments
  • •Extra Healing — +5% health recovery per turn
  • •Resist Status Ailments — 10% chance to resist debuffs
  • •Increase Attack Buffs — Attack buffs are 8% more effective
  • •Attack Ailment Reduction — Attack ailments are 8% less effective
  • •Increase Defense Buffs — Defense buffs are 8% more effective
  • •Defense Ailment Reduction — Defense ailments are 8% less effective

How to Get Legendary Troops

Legendary troops can be obtained in two ways:

1. Legendary Troop Summon — A dedicated summon portal that appears every ~2 weeks. Costs 300 gems per summon (or 2600 for 10x) or Legendary Troop Tokens. The summon rates are: 83% Rare (3★), 14% Epic (4★), and 3% Legendary (5★).

2. Epic Troop Conversion — In the Barracks, you can convert a max-level Epic (4★) troop into a Legendary troop. This costs Iron and Class Emblems matching the legendary troop's primary class. There is a 28-day cooldown between conversions. The resulting legendary troop will be the same element as the epic troop used.

Conversion is the most reliable path to specific legendary troops, as summoning is completely random. Most experienced players convert their duplicate crit or mana troops once they have leveled replacements.

Legendary Troops FAQ

How do I convert an epic troop to a legendary troop?

In the Barracks, select a high level (26+) Epic troop and choose conversion. Costs Iron + Class Emblems. 28-day cooldown between conversions.

Are legendary troops better than epic troops?

When class-matched, yes. Legendary troops with class synergy provide significantly higher stats plus a passive skill that epic troops lack.

What class emblems do I need for legendary troop conversion?

Emblems matching the primary class of the target legendary troop. Every 10 levels also requires additional emblems.

How many legendary troops are there in Empires and Puzzles?

There are 100 legendary troops total — 20 per element (10 Set 1 + 10 Set 2), covering all 10 class pairings per set.

Which legendary troop should I level first?

Prioritize the troop whose primary class matches your best hero in that element. The class synergy bonus is what makes legendaries shine.
