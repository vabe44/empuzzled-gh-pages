Gongoth: The Minion-Mashing Astral Annihilator

Overview

Gongoth is a 5-star Legendary Dark (Purple) hero from the Astral Dwarfs family. Released in May 2025, he quickly established himself as a premier damage dealer designed to punish the heavy minion and mega-minion meta that has dominated the game. As a Barbarian, he has the potential to inflict bleed damage, but his real threat lies in his Fast mana speed and a Special Skill that scales violently against summoner-heavy teams. While he lacks a costume, his base form is potent enough to make him a terrifying presence on both offense and defense. If you ever wanted a dwarf who looks like he could bench press a dragon while simultaneously insulting its mother, Gongoth is your guy.

Hero Art & Visuals

Gongoth sports a classic "space dwarf" aesthetic, combining heavy, ornate armor with cosmic purple hues that scream "Astral Plane." His design features a magnificent, braided white beard adorned with glowing purple gems, and a helmet that looks both ceremonial and practical for skull-crushing. He wields a massive weapon that looks like a cross between a mace and a pickaxe, perfect for cracking open heavy armor or, in his case, smashing through minions. The art style fits perfectly within the Astral Dwarfs family, emphasizing sturdy builds and magical, glowing accents.

Hero Stats

With a base Power of 1184, Gongoth sits comfortably in the upper echelon of modern stats, though slightly behind the bleeding edge of heroes released in late 2025 (like the 1230+ Power heroes from the Temple Summon). His Attack stat of 1229 is impressive, making him a heavy hitter, while his Health (2092) and Defense (1203) ensure he isn't made of glass. When compared to contemporaries, he holds his own very well. He is stats-heavy enough to survive the initial volley in wars and fast enough to retaliate before he goes down.

Special Skill: Twilight Star Strike

Analysis: "Twilight Star Strike" is a devastating skill. Hitting three targets for 400% damage at Fast speed is already top-tier territory. Historically, hitting three enemies this hard was reserved for Average or Slow heroes, or those with significant drawbacks.

The secondary effects push him over the edge.

  1. Wither: Reducing the target and nearby enemies' Attack by -350 is a massive survival tool. It cripples the enemy's ability to kill your team with slash attacks or special skills.
  2. Damage Amplification: The +25% increased damage received is a solid "soft" defense down. However, the scaling mechanic (+10% per minion/mega minion up to +85%) is where he shines. Against a team protecting themselves with minions (like the Cookie family or other summoners), Gongoth turns their defense into a liability.

By the Numbers

  • Mana Speed: Fast. He charges in 8 tiles, or 7 with a level 29 mana troop/magic troop and emblem bonuses.
  • Damage Potential:
    • Base Hit: 1229 Attack x 400% = ~4916 raw damage value per target.
    • Total output: ~14,748 raw damage spread across three targets.
  • Damage Amplification:
    • Base: +25% damage taken by enemies.
    • Max Condition (6+ minions on enemy team): +85% damage taken. This effectively nearly doubles the damage they take from subsequent hits.
  • Wither: -350 Attack is a flat reduction. Against a modern hero with ~1500 Attack, this is roughly a 23% reduction in their offensive power.

Family Bonus, Passives, Aether Power & Unique Abilities

Family Bonus

The Astral Dwarf family bonus is excellent for disruption. A 50% chance (for 2+ heroes) to dispel a buff from all enemies at the end of the turn is a passive dispel that can ruin the enemy's rhythm without you doing anything. More importantly for Gongoth, the innate resistance to mana reduction (up to 100% with 2+ heroes) makes him a hard counter to mana controllers like Fergus or Waterpipe. Gaining mana when resisting is the cherry on top.

Passives

  • Superior Bypass: Bypassing minions and mega minions ensures his 400% hit connects directly with the hero's health pool. This is vital for a finisher.
  • Superior Resist: He takes less damage from minions, increasing his tankiness against summoner teams.
  • Perfect Accuracy: He never misses. This completely negates Blind effects (like from Drake Fong or Justice) and Dodge effects (like from Constance or Rochefort). He is reliable.

Aether Power

Special Boost gives significantly more damage on his special skill for the first 6 turns. Since he is Fast, he is very likely to fire within this window, making his opening strike absolutely nuclear.

Legacy & Modern Relevance

  • Historical Context: Upon release in May 2025, Gongoth was hailed as a "Fast speed monster" who broke the mold for hit-3 damage dealers. He was seen as a direct answer to the Mega Minion meta.
  • Power Creep Analysis: By December 2025, while new heroes like Devyani have slightly higher base stats, Gongoth's 400% multiplier at Fast speed remains incredibly competitive. The "ceiling" for fast splash damage hasn't moved much past him.
  • Modern Viability Verdict: Gongoth remains a top-tier PvP hero. He is not just a relic; he is a staple in attacking war teams and a very dangerous flank on defense. His specific counter-mechanics (Anti-Minion, Anti-Dodge, Anti-Mana Control) ensure he will stay relevant even as raw stats creep higher.

Costume & Costume Bonus

Gongoth currently does not have a costume. His base version is strong enough to stand alone without needing a costume bonus to prop up his stats.

Emblem Path Analysis

Gongoth is a Barbarian.

The Aggressor (Recommended)

  • Path: Attack > Defense > Health
  • Pros: Maximizes the 400% damage multiplier and the bleed damage from the Barbarian talent.
  • Cons: Slightly lower HP pool makes him vulnerable to snipers.

The Survivor

  • Path: Defense > Health > Attack
  • Pros: Ensures he lives long enough to fire his skill, applying the crucial Wither and Damage Increase debuffs.
  • Cons: Sacrifices kill pressure on his primary hit.

Final Recommendation

For most players, the Aggressor path (Attack focus) is recommended. Gongoth's primary job is to kill or severely maim three enemies at once. The faster he kills, the less damage your team takes.

Strengths

  • Massive Fast Damage: 400% damage to three targets at Fast speed is elite.
  • Minion Counter: Bypasses minions and scales damage based on their presence.
  • Reliability: Perfect Accuracy means he ignores Dodge and Blind.
  • Support & Survival: Wither reduces incoming damage, and the damage increase debuff sets up teammates for kills.
  • Mana Control Resistance: Immune to mana cuts (with family bonus), protecting his timing.

Weaknesses

  • Hit-3 Targeting: Like all Hit-3 heroes, he becomes less effective if the tank dies and the enemies are split (hitting wings only).
  • Cleanse Vulnerability: The Wither and Damage Increase debuffs can be cleansed by supports or specific passive abilities (like some Garrison Guards).
  • Glass Cannon Potential: If not emblemed for defense, he can be taken out by a strong sniper before firing.

Best Game Modes

  • PvP Raids & War (Offense): His best role. Use him to break stubborn defenses, especially those relying on minions or dodge.
  • PvP Defense: A solid Flank. His Fast speed puts immediate pressure on attackers.
  • Tournaments: Excellent in Rush Attack (Very Fast) and Buff Booster (high damage).
  • Titans: Usable due to high attack and Wither (survival), though not a specialist.

Advanced Strategy & Team Compositions

Sample Team Ideas

  1. The Purple Smash:
    • Team: Tiburtus (C2) / Gongoth / Bastet / Becky / Rigard (C2)
    • Concept: Defense Down followed by Gongoth's massive hit and Becky's follow-up.
  2. The Anti-Minion Squad:
    • Team: Gongoth / Grimsteel / / /
    • Concept: Using Gongoth alongside other minion counters to dismantle summoner teams like Waterpipe or the Cookie family.

Counters & Anti-Synergies

  • Counters: He hates heroes who reflect Purple (Yellow Titans/Heroes with elemental reflect) or those who prevent status ailments (Aron, Hathor).
  • Anti-Synergy: Be careful overlapping his "Damage Increase" debuff with other similar effects if they don't stack (e.g., Gastille), as they might overwrite each other rather than adding up.

Advice for New Players

If you pulled Gongoth early, congratulations. He is a carry hero. Focus on maxing him immediately. He will clear map stages easily with his multi-hit and keep your team alive with Wither. Don't worry too much about the minion scaling mechanic early on; 400% damage is enough to kill almost anything in the early game.

Advice for Veterans

Gongoth is worth the Limit Break and Alpha Aethers. His "Special Boost" aether power synergizes perfectly with his Fast speed. In the current meta of heavy stats and damage reduction, having a hero who not only bypasses minions but uses them to amplify damage is a key tactical advantage. He remains a primary weapon against the annoying "Stall and Minion" defenses.

Community Corner: Hot Takes & History

When released, the community was shocked by the 400% number on a Fast hero.

  • "A bit too strong for fast, don't you think? It should actually be Medium!" was a common sentiment.
  • Players noted his synergy against the "Mega Minion" meta, calling him an "Astral Annihilator."
  • Some debate existed regarding his survivability, with players noting, "I found testing... that his lowish health is noticed... he basically needs to get 2 shots off."
  • Comparison to Burton was frequent, with players feeling Gongoth offered way more utility (Wither + Dmg Increase) without the drawback of Insanity.

Final Thoughts

Gongoth is a powerhouse. He represents the modern era of "Fast" heroes where speed does not necessitate a sacrifice in power. By combining high burst damage, defensive utility via Wither, and specific anti-meta mechanics (Anti-Minion/Anti-Dodge), he secures his place as one of the best Dark attackers in the game. He isn't just a blunt instrument; he is a tactical nuke.

Verdict: An essential hero for any competitive roster.

Note: This review is based on the hero's state as of December 2025.
