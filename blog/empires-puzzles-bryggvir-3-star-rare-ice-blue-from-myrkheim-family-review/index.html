Bryggvir, The Dwarven Goat Rider: Frosted Tips and Mana Sips

Overview

Bryggvir enters the arena as a 3-star Rare hero from the Untold Tales 2 (Myrkheim) family, released in late August 2025. While many eyes are usually glued to the flashy 5-star legends, this little dwarf riding a goat commands attention in the Rare rarity bracket. He serves as a hybrid damage dealer and support unit, offering a potent combination of multi-target damage and mana acceleration. He is essentially a pocket-sized powerhouse designed to dominate 3-star tournaments and Challenge Events.

Hero Art & Visuals

The artwork for Bryggvir is undeniably charming and full of character. It features a joyous dwarf wielding a crystalline ice sword, mounted atop a rather enthusiastic-looking goat. The aesthetic screams "Hog Rider" from other popular strategy games but with a distinct frosty, Nordic twist. The goat's expression suggests it is just as ready for battle as its rider, adding a touch of humor to the otherwise serious Myrkheim theme. It is refreshing to see such personality packed into a 3-star card.

Hero Stats

Bryggvir's stats are exceptional for a 3-star hero. With a base power of 515 and limit broken stats pushing his Attack towards 800, he sits comfortably at the top of the modern Rare hero food chain. His stat distribution is fairly balanced but leans slightly towards Health and Attack, making him sturdy enough to survive a few hits while ensuring his special skill dents the opposition. Compared to classic 3-stars like Valen or even older event heroes, Bryggvir is statistically superior in every metric.

Special Skill: Goat Rush

Goat Rush is a multi-faceted skill that provides immense value at Average speed.

  1. Damage: It hits the target and nearby enemies. The text mentions "dealing up to 840 damage," which suggests a raw damage calculation that scales, likely with the growth/wither mechanics introduced in recent sagas, or simply represents a very high base damage for this rarity. Against Fire enemies, this damage is amplified by 20%, making him a red-tank killer.
  2. Utility: Crucially, the attack never misses. This ignores Dodge buffs (like those from Rogue talents or specific heroes) and Blindness, ensuring consistent output.
  3. Support: The skill grants 10% mana to the caster and nearby allies. This is a game-changer in 3-star formats, where mana control is scarce.

By the Numbers

Let us look at the mechanics behind the goat.

  • Mana Speed Analysis: At Average speed, Bryggvir requires 10 tiles to charge. With his own 10% mana boost upon firing, he effectively shortens his next charge cycle. In Rush Attack tournaments, this mana gain is even more oppressive, allowing his team to cycle specials relentlessly.
  • Damage Calculation: The "840 damage" cap indicates he can nearly one-shot standard 3-star heroes who often hover around 800-1000 HP. Even if this damage is split or mitigated by defense, hitting three targets for substantial damage at the 3-star level is potent.
  • Mana Breakpoints: The 10% mana boost is significant. For Average speed allies, this can often bridge the gap between needing one more ghosted tile or being ready to fire. It essentially functions like a mini-Xnolphod effect for the Rare bracket.

Family Bonus, Passives, Aether Power & Unique Abilities

The Myrkheim family brings a complex set of passives that elevates Bryggvir beyond a simple damage dealer.

Family Bonus

(Note: While the specific family bonus percentages are standard for Untold Tales, the real power lies in the Realm effects and Passives below.)

Passives

Arctic Core: This is the defining feature of the Myrkheim heroes.

  • Every time Bryggvir casts his special, the Core activates.
  • It deals frost damage over 3 turns and lowers the target's attack by 15% per turn (Corrosive Frost).
  • The damage scales with the "charge" of the core (which builds up during the battle). A fully charged core deals significantly more damage.
  • It deals extra damage to Minion/Mega Minion owners.
  • Analysis: This passive adds a layer of Damage over Time (DoT) and attack mitigation that is rare in 3-star heroes. It softens up the enemy team while reducing their return fire, effectively increasing your team's survivability.

Empowered Resist Frost:

  • He is immune to Frost damage.
  • If he resists it, he gains 450 Boosted Health and 5% Mana.
  • Analysis: This is a hard counter to other Myrkheim heroes or any water-themed seasonal heroes that deal Frost DoT. Gaining 450 HP (which is nearly half his health bar) simply for resisting an ailment is incredible sustain.

Aether Power

Dodge: The Aether Power gives him a chance to dodge special skills for the first 6 turns. This adds a layer of survivability that helps ensure he fires his skill at least once, which is often all you need to swing a 3-star match.

Legacy & Modern Relevance

  • Historical Context: 3-star metas evolve slower than 5-star metas, but the introduction of "Growth/Wither" mechanics and "Cores" to this rarity marks a significant leap.
  • Power Creep Analysis: Bryggvir represents the current peak of 3-star power creep. His stats dwarf Season 1 heroes, and his skill complexity (Direct Damage + Never Miss + Mana Boost + Passive DoT + Passive Attack Down) is something previously reserved for 5-star heroes.
  • Modern Viability Verdict: He is a top-tier contender. In the current landscape of late 2025, he is indispensable for competitive 3-star tournament players and those aiming for high scores in the Rare tier of Challenge Events.

Emblem Path Analysis

As a Barbarian, Bryggvir has access to the wound effect, which is nice but not the main draw. The focus should be on ensuring he survives to fire his mana boost or maximizing his hit damage.

The Aggressor (Recommended)

  • Path: Attack > Defense > Health
  • Pros: Maximizes the "Frostfall Strike" damage and the Arctic Core damage output.
  • Cons: Slightly squishier, though his base stats are already high.

The Survivor

  • Path: Defense > Health > Attack
  • Pros: Keeps him alive longer to act as a mana battery for his flanks.
  • Cons: Sacrifices the kill potential of his special skill.

Final Recommendation

For most players, the Aggressor (Attack) path is recommended. In 3-star battles, alpha-striking (killing the enemy before they kill you) is generally the best strategy, and Bryggvir's damage potential is too good to ignore.

Strengths

  • Mana Generation: +10% mana to 3 heroes is a massive tempo swinger.
  • Reliability: The "Never Miss" attribute is crucial against Rogue-heavy defenses.
  • Versatility: He damages, he buffs mana, he applies DoT, and he debuffs attack.
  • Anti-Minion: His passive deals extra damage to minion summoners, who are common in modern defenses.

Weaknesses

  • Speed: Average speed can be a liability in very fast-paced matches if the board is bad, though his mana boost helps mitigate this once he fires.
  • Passive Specificity: His resistance passive is useless if the enemy does not deal Frost damage.

Best Game Modes

  • Raid Tournaments (Rush Attack): He will be an absolute monster here. Firing a hit-3 nuke that boosts mana at Very Fast speed is devastating.
  • Raid Tournaments (Bloody Battle): His high damage and lack of reliance on healing (aside from his self-boost passive) make him a strong pick.
  • Challenge Events: His high damage cap and mana boost can speed up wave clearing in the Rare tier.
  • Buff Booster Tournaments: While he doesn't buff stats per se, the mana boost helps buffers fire faster.

Advanced Strategy & Team Compositions

Bryggvir works best when positioned to maximize his mana boost. He hits the target and nearby, and buffs the caster and nearby. This means he affects himself and his two neighbors.

Sample Team Ideas

  1. The Blue Rush: Nordri - Bryggvir - Gato/Karil (C). Nordri applies Elemental Defense Down, Bryggvir hits and boosts mana, and the sniper finishes them off.
  2. The Mana Engine: Faiez - Bryggvir - Treevil. In Rush wars, this combination is toxic. Faiez protects, Bryggvir speeds everyone up, and Treevil devastates the board.
  3. The Event Blaster: Buster - Bryggvir - Zarola. Buster increases damage taken, Bryggvir softens the wave and boosts mana, allowing Zarola to snipe rapidly.

Counters & Anti-Synergies

  • Mana Controllers: Heroes like Pixie or Bauchan who reduce mana can stop Bryggvir from starting his engine.
  • Counters: Boril (at 4-star) or similar riposte heroes in mixed wars can be dangerous due to his high AoE damage.
  • Synergy Note: Do not place him on the wing (edges). He needs to be in position 2, 3, or 4 to give his mana boost to two allies instead of just one.

Advice for New Players

If you pull Bryggvir, level him immediately. He is far superior to standard 3-star blue heroes like Graymane or Valen. He will serve as your tank or flank in 3-star tournaments for a long time.

Advice for Veterans

You might overlook 3-stars, but do not sleep on this one for your tournament roster. He is worth the Limit Break and potentially the Alpha Aether if you are serious about ranking in tournaments. His "Hog Rider" aesthetic alone makes him a fun collection piece, but his utility is what keeps him relevant.

Community Corner: Hot Takes & History

The community reaction has been overwhelmingly positive, with many players drawing comparisons to the "Hog Rider" from Clash Royale due to the visual similarities.

  • "He is basically a mini-Xnolphod with that mana boost."
  • "Finally, a 3-star that punishes the Fire meta in tournaments."
  • "The never-miss is the sleeper OP part of his kit against all these C-Tyrums and Rogues."

Final Thoughts

Bryggvir is a delightful addition to the 3-star roster. He combines a fun design with a kit that is deadly serious. The combination of unmissable damage and mana acceleration is a recipe for success in Empires & Puzzles. While he won't be slaying Titans or fighting in Diamond raids, he is the king of the kiddie pool.

Verdict: A top-tier 3-star hero who brings heavy weaponry to a knife fight. Level him, emblem him, and enjoy the ride.
