Lionstring: The Very Fast Tailor Who Stitches Up Healers

Overview

Lionstring arrived in March 2024 as part of the glitzy Astral Summon, bringing a touch of haute couture to the battlefield. As a Nature (Green) Epic Wizard, he stands out not for brute force, but for his blistering Very Fast mana speed and a utility belt that includes dodging and petty theft (of healing, that is). In a world of hulking barbarians and cosmic dragons, Lionstring is just a dressmaker with a measuring tape and a dreamâ€”and surprisingly, that's often enough to ruin an opponent's day in 4-Star tournaments.

Hero Art & Visuals

Lionstringâ€™s visual design is delightfully distinct. He appears to be a cosmic tailor or dressmaker, draped in fabric that swirls like the night sky. He wields needles and measuring tape rather than a sword or staff, giving him a non-threatening appearance that belies his annoying skill set. Itâ€™s a refreshing break from the "angry warrior screaming at the sky" trope. He looks like heâ€™s about to critique your defense teamâ€™s outfit coordination before dodging your attacks.

Hero Stats

Lionstringâ€™s stats were solid for early 2024 but face a steep uphill battle in late 2025.

Stat Analysis: Compared to modern 4-star behemoths like Breca (released Dec 2025), Lionstring feels a bit threadbare.

  • Lionstring (Max Power): 697
  • Breca (Max Power): 794
  • Modern Gap: Breca boasts over 100 more points in Attack and Defense, and a massive 200+ point lead in Health. To keep Lionstring relevant, you absolutely need to Limit Break him (LB1 and likely LB2). Without the stat boost, he risks being one-shot by modern snipers before he can even lift his needle.

Special Skill: Cloth of Constellations

Skill Analysis: "Cloth of Constellations" is a skill that thrives on disruption rather than destruction.

  • Speed is King: At Very Fast speed, Lionstring charges in just 6.5 tiles (6 tiles with a small mana boost). This allows him to fire early and often, setting up his defensive buffs before the enemy can properly engage.
  • The Dodge: Granting a 45% Dodge chance to himself and nearby allies is his most potent ability. While it only lasts 2 turns, the Very Fast speed means you can keep it active frequently. It forces enemies to gamble with their specials.
  • Healing Steal: Stealing 50% of healing is a niche but hilarious mechanic. It turns the enemy's healers into partial batteries for your team. Itâ€™s particularly rude against heal-over-time (HoT) heroes.
  • Damage: The 245% damage to the target (and minor to nearby) is negligible by 2025 standards. Don't rely on him to kill; rely on him to annoy.

By the Numbers

  • Mana Speed: Very Fast. With a level 11 Mana Troop (or even lower with costume/emblem bonuses if applicable, though he has no costume), he hits the 6-tile breakpoint easily.
  • Damage Potential: 245% of ~684 Attack is roughly 1,675 damage value (before defense). Against modern defenses with 1000+ defense, this will likely result in "chip damage"â€”perhaps 200-300 actual damage. He is not a killer.
  • Defensive Value: The 45% Dodge effectively gives his team (well, 3 of them) nearly a coin-flip chance to ignore huge hits like Devyani's 500% snipe.

The Astral Elf family kit is designed for survival and disruption, making them slippery targets.

Aether Power

Analysis: Dodge. This stacks thematically with his special skill. For the first 6 turns, he has a passive chance to dodge specials. If his special skill is also active, he becomes incredibly elusive, frustrating enemies who just want to pin him down.

Legacy & Modern Relevance

  • Historical Context: Upon release, Lionstring was hailed as a top-tier 4-star, often compared to a mini-Kadilen (Costume) due to the dodge. He was a staple in 4-Star Buff Booster tournaments.
  • Power Creep Analysis: The damage numbers have fallen off a cliff. 245% damage is now what some healers do. However, Very Fast Dodge is a mechanic that ages gracefully. Even if he does zero damage, making a teammate dodge a kill-shot is valuable.
  • Modern Viability Verdict: Lionstring remains Highly Viable in specific niches: 4-Star Tournaments (especially Rush and Buff Booster) and War Cleanup teams. He is no longer a general-purpose raid hero, but in restricted formats, his speed and utility still shine.

Costume & Costume Bonus

As of late 2025, Lionstring does not have a costume. He must rely on his base kit and Limit Breaks to keep up with the stat inflation of newer heroes.

Emblem Path Analysis

Lionstring is a support hero disguised as a wizard. His damage is secondary; staying alive to cast Dodge is primary.

The Survivor (Recommended)

  • Path: Defense > Health > Attack
  • Pros: Maximizes his survivability. With "Share Damage" and low base stats, he needs every point of armor to avoid being the weak link.
  • Cons: His special skill hit becomes even softer, essentially just a tickle.

The Glass Needle

  • Path: Attack > Defense > Health
  • Pros: Tries to make his Very Fast hit count for something, utilizing the Wizard Jinx talent for extra damage.
  • Cons: He becomes dangerously fragile and may die before firing his second loop of Dodge.

Final Recommendation

For most players, the Survivor (Defense/Health) path is recommended. You bring him for the Dodge and the Cure-Steal, not the damage.

Strengths

  • Speed: Very Fast mana allows him to dictate the pace of the match.
  • Survivability Support: Dodge is one of the strongest defensive buffs in the game.
  • Healing Denial: Stealing healing is an excellent counter to heavy healer teams, effectively functioning as a "reverse heal."
  • Never Misses: Reliable against Dodge or Blind heavy teams.

Weaknesses

  • Low Stats: severely outclassed by late 2025 heroes in raw numbers.
  • Short Duration: The buffs/debuffs only last 2 turns. You must keep firing to maintain them.
  • Weak Damage: He effectively contributes zero offensive pressure against modern tanks.
  • Dispel Vulnerability: His Dodge is a buff, making him vulnerable to dispellers (like Sabina or Melendor) who can strip his protection instantly.

Best Game Modes

  • 4-Star Tournaments (Buff Booster): His Dodge counts as a buff, and his speed allows him to stack it quickly.
  • 4-Star Tournaments (Rush Attack): While already Very Fast, Rush makes him even more oppressive, though his relative speed advantage is diminished.
  • War Cleanups: Good for protecting a team of leftovers against a partially killed enemy defense.

Advanced Strategy & Team Compositions

Lionstring is a "protector" flank. He should sit next to your tank or your key glass cannon to keep them safe.

Sample Team Ideas

  • The 4-Star Dodge City: Lionstring - Goldie - Caedmon (Toon).
    • Why: Goldie reflects ailments, Lionstring provides Dodge, and Caedmon cleanses/dispels. A very annoying defensive core for tournaments.
  • The Healer Stealer: Lionstring - Gato - Proteus.
    • Why: Proteus stops mana, Lionstring steals any passive healing, and Gato provides immunity. Good for controlling boss fights.

Counters & Anti-Synergies

  • Counters: Dispellers (Sabina, Melendor, Mist) are his worst nightmare. If they strip his Dodge, he is just a squishy elf with a needle. Superior Wizards can also dispel his buffs easily.
  • Anti-Synergy: Other Dodge Heroes. Don't pair him with another Dodge hero (like C. Kadilen in War) as the buffs will overwrite each other, wasting efficiency.

Advice for New Players

Lionstring is an excellent pull. He offers a pseudo-5-star ability (Dodge) at a 4-star cost. Level him immediately for your tournament teams and War depth. He will save your team more often than you realize.

Advice for Veterans

You likely have 5-star heroes who do this better (Constance, Ukkonen, etc.). However, Lionstring is still a king in the 4-star arena. If you care about tournament loot, he is worth the emblems and LB1. Save the Alpha Aethers (LB2) for heroes with more timeless stats unless you are a tournament specialist.

Community Corner: Hot Takes & History

When released, the community was pleasantly surprised.

  • The F2P Gem: "This guy is the F2P 5* from this portal," one player noted, highlighting his high-value skill set for a 4-star.
  • The Valoria Comparison: "Valeria cries in the corner," joked another, noting how Lionstring's healing steal outclassed older mechanics.
  • The "Very Fast" Hype: "Not so many 4* Very Fast heroes. Dodge, share dmg with others, steal of the healing - seems like a nice tank for tourneys."

Final Thoughts

Lionstring is a prime example of "utility over power." He won't win any arm-wrestling contests against the steroid-fueled heroes of late 2025, but he doesn't have to. He just has to be faster than them. With his ability to weave a protective cloak of stars around your team before the enemy can blink, he remains a staple of the discerning player's 4-star roster.

Verdict: A fragile but incredibly useful utility hero who remains a top pick for 4-star tournaments despite aging stats.
